## ✅ MAIN POST (copy–paste this)

Meet **Kintora** — an app I built to help people *never lose the moments and the people who matter most.* 💜

Here's the honest story of how it started 👇

It began as a simple conversation. My coworker **Albert Catorsi** and I were just talking about AI — Codex, Claude Code, where all of this is heading. And we asked ourselves a real question: *"What app could we build that actually makes an impact and solves a real problem?"*

Albert had the spark: a **memory vault.** His first thought was for people living with Alzheimer's — a place to safeguard their memories. But the more I sat with it, the more I realized… this isn't just for one group of people. **This is for everyone.** All of us forget names, dates, faces, stories, the little things that quietly slip away.

So we took the idea straight into **Claude Code (Opus 4.8)** and described it out loud. In about **20 minutes**, we had a working mockup design. 🤯

But here's the part I really want to be honest about: AI didn't build this *for* me. Opus 4.8 is an incredibly smart model — it can turn an idea into a real application fast — **but it still needs a person who actually understands how software is built** to shape it, fix it, secure it, and push it to something real. That's where I come in. I'm a software developer, and I spent the time improving the idea, refining the use cases, and hardening it into something I'd actually trust with my own family's memories.

And that's how **Kintora** was born.

---

🔒 **What makes Kintora different: it's 100% offline.**
Nothing leaves your computer. No cloud. No account. No tracking. No internet required. Your memories stay *yours.*

---

✨ **What you can do with Kintora:**
👥 **People** — remember faces, names, relationships, and birthdays
📷 **Memories** — capture moments with photos *and voice notes*
⭐ **Important Info** — keep the facts that matter close
🗓️ **Timeline** — see your life's story laid out
🕸️ **Connections** — explore how everyone & everything links together in a 3D graph
🌅 **Recall Mode** — a calm, large-text daily briefing that can even *read itself aloud* to you
💾 **Backup & Restore** — one click to keep everything safe on a USB or another PC

---

💡 **Who it's for:**
- Someone preparing for, or living with, Alzheimer's or memory loss
- Caregivers and families who want to preserve a loved one's story
- Honestly… **anyone** who wants a private place to hold onto what matters

This started as a 20-minute conversation between two coworkers. It's now a real desktop app I'm proud of.

Built with care, to hold on to what matters. 💜

## 💼 OPTIONAL — LinkedIn / professional angle (same story, dev-focused)

A few weeks ago, a casual conversation with my coworker **Albert Catorsi** about AI tooling (Codex, Claude Code) turned into a real product.

The question we asked: what could we build that solves a genuine problem? His answer — a **memory vault**, originally for people living with Alzheimer's. I expanded the scope: everyone forgets things that matter.

We prototyped in **Claude Code (Opus 4.8)** and had a working mockup in ~20 minutes. But I want to be clear about something the hype often skips: **AI accelerates a skilled developer — it doesn't replace one.** Turning that mockup into **Kintora** took real engineering: an offline-first architecture, an Electron desktop build, a native SQLite data layer, safe backup/restore, and careful privacy decisions (I deliberately rejected cloud speech-to-text to keep it 100% offline).

**Stack:** Angular 20 + Tailwind, Node/Express, better-sqlite3, packaged as a Windows desktop app with Electron. Fully offline — no cloud, no accounts, no telemetry.

The lesson for me: the best results come from **AI + domain knowledge + engineering discipline**, not AI alone.
